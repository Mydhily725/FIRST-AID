What is Seizures?

A seizure is a sudden and temporary disturbance in the electrical activity of the brain. This disruption can cause a variety of physical and mental symptoms, ranging from mild and brief episodes to more severe, life-threatening conditions. Seizures occur when a group of neurons (nerve cells) in the brain fire electrical signals abnormally, either in a localized area (focal seizures) or across the whole brain (generalized seizures). The frequency, duration, and severity of seizures can vary widely between individuals and may also depend on the underlying cause.
