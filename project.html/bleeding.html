BLEEDING

What is Bleeding?

Bleeding, also referred to as hemorrhage, is the loss of blood from the circulatory system. It can occur internally, when blood escapes from blood vessels within the body, or externally, when blood leaks out through a break in the skin or mucous membranes. Bleeding is a critical condition, as it can lead to significant health complications if not managed promptly. The severity of bleeding is influenced by factors such as the location of the injury, the amount of blood lost, and the individual’s overall health.


Types of Bleeding
