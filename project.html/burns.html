What is Burns?

Burns are injuries to the body caused by exposure to heat, chemicals, electricity, or radiation, leading to tissue damage. They are classified based on their severity, depth, and cause. Burns can range from mild, superficial injuries to severe, life-threatening conditions. The seriousness of a burn depends on its depth, the area of the body affected, and the duration of exposure to the damaging agent.
